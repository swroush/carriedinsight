About Sharon

I've spent two decades navigating the investment landscape across investment banking, private equity, and institutional capital allocation at a large family office. I've evaluated hundreds of investment teams and worked alongside leaders making consequential decisions where capital, people, and outcomes were all on the line. That perspective is what I bring to coaching.

My work is for investors and senior leaders who want a confidential, deeply thoughtful space to work through the questions that matter most.

My work is shaped by three sources of experience.

Two decades of investing across cycles have shaped how I think about conviction, uncertainty, and the endurance required to lead well over time. As a CFA charterholder, I'm comfortable in the details. Our conversations begin with a shared understanding of the markets and the realities you operate in. My coaching is grounded in evidence-based practice and informed by neuroscience, drawing on my training through Columbia.

I live just outside New York City and spend a good part of my time raising my son and training for marathons, both of which continue to shape how I think about endurance, pacing, and sustained performance.
